Frequently Asked Questions

Cardivascular Disease

  • Q: What is Cardivascular disease?
    •  A: Cardivascular disease concerns anything to do with the heart and bloodvessels.

  • Q: What are common forms Cardivasular disease?
    •  A:Common forms of the disease include:
    • Ischemic Heart Disease (IHD)
    • Stroke
    • Hypertensive Heart Disease
    • Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD)
    • Aortic Aneurysms
    • Cardiomyopathy
    • Atrial Fibrillation
    • Congenital Heart Disease
    • Endocarditis
    • Peripheral Atery Disease (PAD)

Health

  • Q: What can I do to prevent cardiovascular disease?
    •  A: A simple yet very effective way is to maintain a healthy lifestyle:

      This means being physically active. By maintaining a healthy body weight you can drastically reduce your risk of cardiovascular disease. Simple excersise include:

    • Regular walking, such as to work or school instead of driving
    • Try getting a group of friends to form a team, as teamsports is an easy way to motivate you into doing sports.

    Maintain a healthy diet:

      By adopting healthy eating habits you can easily lower the risks of cardiovascular disease. Follow these simple yet effective types towards healthier eating:

    • Substitute fried and fatty foods for fresh produce, like fruit and vegetables
    • Reduce serving sizes to avoid overeating
    • Drink water rather than sugary drinks

Symptoms to look out for

  • Q: What are the symptoms of high blood pressure?
    •  A: The symptoms for constricted blood vessels(atherosclerotic disease) are:
    • Chest pain(angina)
    • Shortness of breath
    • Pain, numbness, weakness or coldness in your legs or arms if the blood vessels in those parts of your body are narrowed
    • Pain in the neck, jaw, throat, upper abdomen or back

  • Q: What are the symptoms of heart arrhythmias
    •  A: The symptoms for heart arrhythmias(irregular heartbeats) include:
    • Fluttering in your chest
    • Racing heartbeat (tachycardia)
    • Slowed heartbeat (bradychardia)
    • Chest pain or discomfort
    • Shortness of breath
    • Lightheadedness
    • Dizziness
    • Fainting(syncope) or near fainting
